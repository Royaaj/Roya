Green Building

Green building is the practice of developing buildings that are environmentally responsible and resource-efficient throughout a building's life cycle.

It is also known as sustainable building, expands and compliments the building design concerns of economy, utility, durability, and comfort.

Advocating for Green Building Engineering and Green Architecture is encouraged worldwide to minimize and solve numerous environmental prombles, specifically in urban areas. This initiative leads the way towards incorporating generally accepted rating system in order to achieve the goals and objectives of green building engineering.

Objectives

Reduce overall impact on human health and the natural environment by:

  • Using energy water, and other resources efficiently.
  • Reducing waste, pollution, and environmental degradation.
  • Providing healthier space for occupants as compared to conventional buildings.
They incorporate various features and technologies to achieve sustainability goals and will generally include energy-efficient appliances (HVAC), renewable energy sources (solar), water conservation systems (rainwater harvesting), and environmentally friendly materials.

Benefits

Green buildings can reduce water consumption by 20% to 30% and CO2 emissions by up to 35%.

According to Business Wire, 84% of homeowners say living in a green home is important to them. In addition, 64% say they would pay more to live in a green community.

The main benefit of advocating for green building engineering is maintaing a good environment and minimizing negative environmental impact from urban development through observing different sustainable practices and following the stantard of a sustainable building.
